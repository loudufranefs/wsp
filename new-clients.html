Keeping your pets safe

We strongly recommend pet carriers for cats (and some smaller dogs) and leashes for dogs. The street parking lot can be busy and pets may get very excited by all the activity. If other pets bother your pet, let the client services team know in advance and we can minimize or eliminate the time your pet spends in our reception area.

Examination Process

During the examination and treatment of your pet, a member of the nursing team will assist the doctor. They are trained to safely and comfortably restrain your pet. Most pets will respond better to restraint by someone other than the owner. It works best when the owner remains relaxed and in view of the patient during examination.

After your appointment, our staff can check you out either at the reception counter, or in the exam room. They are also happy to assist getting your pets to and from your car.
